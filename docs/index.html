Methodology · no black boxes

Where every number comes from

The main source

Except for the timeline, every figure is a U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey (ACS) 2020–2024 five-year estimate, pulled directly from the Census API. Five-year estimates are used because they are the most reliable for small subgroups (specific countries of birth, languages, and the like).

Citywide totals use the Census "place" geography for New York city (place 51000); borough figures use the five county equivalents. Medians (age, income, rent, home value) come from the citywide place because medians cannot be summed across boroughs.

The timeline

Section 01 uses decennial census counts, 1790–2020, for the modern five-borough area (U.S. Census Bureau; NYC Department of City Planning historical tables). The jump between 1890 and 1900 reflects the 1898 consolidation of Greater New York, not a real doubling in a decade.

Universes

Denominators vary by table and are noted on each section: total population, population 25+ (education), 16+ (work), 15+ (marital status), workers 16+ (commute), households, or housing units.

Caveats

ACS figures are survey estimates with margins of error, not exact counts; treat small differences as noise. Race and Hispanic origin are shown the way the census asks them — Hispanic/Latino is an origin asked separately from race, so the two breakdowns overlap by design.

Country-of-birth and ancestry lists show the top 20; "ancestry" is self-reported and optional, so its total is smaller than the population.

Build scripts (fetch_acs.py, build_data.py) and the raw data are in the project repository.
